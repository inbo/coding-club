Each game management unit (wildbeheerseenheid, WBE) is required to count partridges in their territory if they want to hunt partridge. There are two dataframes: - `partridge`: a dataset with observations of birds. This includes the date of the observation, the fieldworker who submitted the observation, the type of observation (couple, single, group, or only noise), and location (WBE number and x & y). - `WBE`: for each game management unit (WBE), this dataframe containse the unique identification number (wbe number), name, province, and surface area. GBIF butterfly checklist data, inspired by Dirk's [Eurobutt checklist](https://www.gbif.org/dataset/f9af6ffd-febc-4626-b2e8-809b1c60fa01#description). - `taxon`: dataframe with taxonomic information of butterflies found in West-Europe. Each butterfly has a unique identifier (column `id`) - `vernacularname`: vernacular names in English. Link to `taxon` in column `taxon_id` - `distribution_BE`, `distribution_NL`, `distribution_LU` : data.frames about distribution and threat status of butterflies in Belgium (BE), the Netherlands (NL) and Luxembourg (LU). Link to `taxon` in column `taxon_id` --- background-image: url({{ site.baseurl}}/assets/images/background_challenge_1.png) class: left, middle # Challenge 1 1.
